## Details

### Item 1 — Kimi K2.7-Code (Moonshot AI), released June 12, 2026
- **What changed:** Moonshot shipped an open-weight, coding-specialized successor to K2.6. Architecture: 1T-parameter MoE, 32B active per token, 384 experts (8+1 selected), 256K context, a 400M-param MoonViT vision encoder, mandatory always-on "thinking" mode (non-disableable), locked sampling parameters. Weights are live on Hugging Face (moonshotai/Kimi-K2.7-Code) under a Modified MIT license. API pricing is $0.95 / $4.00 per 1M input/output tokens.
- **Claim quality:** All headline numbers are Moonshot's own proprietary benchmarks (Kimi Code Bench v2, Program Bench, MLS Bench Lite, MCP Atlas, MCPMark Verified). The flagship claims are +21.8% on Kimi Code Bench v2 over K2.6 and ~30% fewer reasoning tokens. As of June 15 there are NO independent third-party numbers on standard public suites (SWE-bench Verified/Pro, LiveCodeBench, Terminal-Bench, GPQA Diamond), and no Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index score yet (for reference, predecessor K2.6 scored 54 on the AA Index).
- **Triage verdict — does NOT clear frontier-threshold:** By Moonshot's own numbers, K2.7-Code trails GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.8 on most coding benchmarks (Kimi Code Bench v2 62.0 vs GPT-5.5 69.0 / Opus 4.8 67.4; MLS Bench Lite 35.1 vs Opus 4.8 81.3). It beats Opus 4.8 only on MCPMark Verified (81.1 vs 76.4) while still trailing GPT-5.5 there (92.9). The real selling point is price (materially cheaper per token than the closed frontier) and token efficiency, not capability.
- **Eval-integrity / benchmark-divergence flag (the reason this is logged):** A developer who builds model routing for the Hermes Agent platform (Sugumaran Balasubramaniyan) publicly challenged the numbers on X — *"Respectfully, every model 'improves' double digits on its own test suite"* — noting that predecessor K2.6 scored only 24% on the independent DeepSWE benchmark (tied with GPT-5.4-mini) while leading on Moonshot's own suites, and that K2.7-Code has not been submitted to DeepSWE. This is a textbook "headline metric without an independent-eval story" — treat the vendor gains as directional, not as a result. Most credible "vs Claude" hands-on reports in circulation are actually about K2.6, and historically show the family underperforming Claude on real end-to-end builds despite strong vendor benchmarks.

### Item 2 — GLM-5.2 (Z.ai / Zhipu AI), released June 13, 2026
- **What changed:** Z.ai's new flagship coding model, released to all GLM Coding Plan tiers (Lite/Pro/Max/Team) with a usable 1M-token context window (model ID glm-5.2[1m], up to 131,072 output tokens) and two thinking-effort levels (High/Max). Underlying architecture is the GLM-5 family's 744B-param MoE with 40B active. It drops into Claude Code / Cline via an Anthropic-compatible endpoint. MIT-licensed open weights, a standalone API, and a chatbot were all promised "next week."
- **Claim quality:** Z.ai published ZERO benchmarks at launch — no SWE-bench, Terminal-Bench, LiveCodeBench, or AA Index. As of June 15, no official GLM-5.2 weights have landed on Hugging Face (the zai-org page lists only through GLM-5.1), and there is no public API or chatbot. The model is therefore untestable from outside the Coding Plan, and there are no credible independent benchmarks or hands-on reports yet. Any specific figure circulating (e.g., an "81.43" score from a promotional GitHub repo) is unreliable and likely a mislabeled GLM-5.1 number.
- **Triage verdict — watch-list pointer, not a logged item.** The significant fact is geopolitical/strategic framing, not capability: GLM-5.2 launched the same day the US government's export-control suspension of Anthropic's Fable 5 / Mythos 5 remained in force, foregrounding a fully-open Chinese frontier-aspirant against a newly-restricted US closed model. Revisit when weights/API and independent benchmarks land (promised week of ~June 16).

### Item 3 — Gemini-SQL2 (Google Research), announced June 12, 2026
- **What changed:** Google Research announced Gemini-SQL2, a text-to-SQL capability built on Gemini 3.1 Pro, reporting 80.04% execution accuracy on the BIRD single-model leaderboard. Google Cloud's prior single-model BIRD record on this track was 76.13% (reported November 15, 2025, per MarkTechPost). Competitor positions cited: AWS Q-SQL ~76.5%, OpenAI GPT-5.5-xhigh ~72.8%, Anthropic Claude Opus 4.6 ~70.9%.
- **Claim quality:** A vendor-announced benchmark via a Google Research thread. No API, model card, or technical report was released, so the result cannot be independently audited or reproduced. It is a capability (specialized post-training/scaffolding on an existing flagship), not a new foundation model.
- **Triage verdict — pointer only.** It's a real, leading benchmark in an economically meaningful task (enterprise text-to-SQL), but it is closed, unauditable, and paper/demo-shaped rather than a deployed-system or independently-measured result. The 12.92-point gap to human performance (80.04% vs human baseline 92.96%, per MarkTechPost) also keeps it below the "remove human review" threshold.

## Trend Pointers (movement, not discrete news)
- **Field openness/closure direction:** Sharpest trend of the window. A US closed frontier model (Mythos-class) sits export-control-suspended while two Chinese labs ship open-weight (Kimi, modified-MIT) or open-weight-promised (GLM-5.2, MIT) coding models the same week. Directional pressure toward Chinese open weights filling the gap left by US closure.
- **Cost-of-intelligence curve:** Continues the ~10x/year decline for equivalent capability that a16z's Guido Appenzeller dubbed "LLMflation" (Nov 2024: *"For an LLM of equivalent performance, the cost is decreasing by 10x every year… What cost $60/million tokens in 2021 costs $.06/million tokens today"*); Epoch AI measures a steeper median ~50x/year decline on some tasks. K2.7-Code and MiniMax M3 (June 1) keep undercutting the closed frontier on price-per-token. No order-of-magnitude in-window event.
- **Agent autonomy / task-length horizon:** Vendor anecdotes (Kimi 300-sub-agent swarms; MiniMax M3 ~24-hour runs with ~2,000 tool calls) keep extending claimed horizons, but these are vendor demos lacking independent reward-hacking-resistant evals — flagged as suspect.
- **Non-NVIDIA / non-CUDA viability:** No in-window event. The DeepSeek-V4-Pro-on-Ascend post-training claim (June 5–6) is prior-run; Huawei's Ascend 950DT (training-class) is reportedly pulled forward to August — a pointer to watch, not yet news.
- **SWE replacement rate:** Continued macro layoff data — Challenger, Gray & Christmas counted exactly 52,050 Technology-sector cuts YTD through March (*"an increase of 40% from the 37,097 cuts… same period last year"*), and TrueUp tracked >142,000 U.S. tech jobs cut in the first five months of 2026 (~33% YoY increase, Oracle's ~30,000-role cut the single largest event). But AI-attribution is far lower than the inflated "~48%" figures some trackers cite: Challenger attributes AI for 15,341 of March's cuts (25% of the month) and 27,645 YTD (~13% of all 2026 cuts). No in-window company-level headcount/output-attribution datapoint of the quality the profile requires. "AI-washing" remains a confound — as Challenger CRO Andy Challenger put it, *"Regardless of whether individual jobs are being replaced by AI, the money for those roles is."*
- **AI-in-science throughput / data-wall:** No in-window AlphaFold-class result. AlphaFold remains the reference case (5-year retrospectives, protein-complex database expansion) but nothing new this window.
- **Industry disruption (finance/law/medicine):** Gemini-SQL2 nudges the enterprise-data-access frontier but is closed and unaudited; no hard-data disruption event.

## Caveats
- This is a deliberately thin set: a 3–4 day window over a weekend produced little signal, which is expected and acceptable. Quality over volume — marginal/hype items were excluded by design.
- All three in-window releases shipped vendor-only or unauditable numbers; none has independent, reward-hacking-resistant evidence as of June 15. Treat every capability claim here as directional.
- Cybersecurity items, ecosystem/protocol/payment-rail news, sub-direction-lead personnel moves, and pure research papers were excluded per scope. The Anthropic June 15 billing/retirement changes are operational, not capability, and are noted only for context.
- Layoff/economic figures come from aggregators (Challenger, Crunchbase, TrueUp) and trackers of varying rigor; "AI-attributed" shares are self-reported and confounded by cost-discipline and "AI-washing."
